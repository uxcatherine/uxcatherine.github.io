The FoodTrace

The Challenge

The FoodTrace is a B2B website that connects food sellers (i.e. farmers and artisans) with food buyers (i.e. restaurants and grocers). Our challenge was to redesign the current beta site, using a mobile-first methodology, to focus the feature set, validate ideas and organize content.

My Role

  • UX Research

  • Information Architecture

  • Interaction Design

Process & Deliverables

In collaboration with my teammate Kaily, I conducted the following:
Domain research, competitive analysis, heuristic evaluation, user interviews, SME interviews, user personas, journey map, task model and wireframes.

Research

During the research phase of the project we conducted domain research and competitive analysis looking at websites that match food sellers and buyers, as well as online marketplaces such as Etsy. We also conducted SME and User Interviews on the phone and in-person at local farmers markets.

Through our research we determined that farmers markets are a key hub of activity for the food industry. This created a particular challenge for us to incorporate a “farmers market feel” into the website.

Following our research synthesis we developed user personas, our problem statement, and design principles.

Problem Statement:
How might we establish and maintain personal connections between food sellers and food buyers on an online platform that creates opportunity for business and relationships?

Wireframes & Prototyping

During our ideation we developed a journey map and task model to identify current pain points and opportunities for FoodTrace. I took the lead on developing the journey map.

Our wireframes highlighted key features and interactions in the beta website including a streamlining of the ‘favourites’ feature, revised terminology, and multiple pictures to incorporate a sense of community and “farmers market feel”.
