PulseBand Health Wearable

The Challenge

The Pulseband is a mock wearable technology that measures a variety of heart health indicators including heart rate, blood pressure, EKG pattern, and respiratory rate. We were challenged with creating a mobile app to accompany this device.

My Role

  • UX Research

  • Information Architecture

  • Interaction Design

Process & Deliverables

In collaboration with my teammates Ji Su (UX Designer) and Kevin (UI Designer), my role included the following:
Competitive analysis, SME interviews, user interviews, usability testing, user testing, user personas, user flows, app map, user scenarios, wireframes, and mid-fidelity prototypes.

Research

I conducted competitive analysis of fitness bands and health apps as well as non-tech items related to heart health such as MedicAlert bracelets. I also conducted SME and user interviews.

One particularly important insight from our research was that blood work (specifically cholesterol information) is necessary for providing an accurate picture of one’s heart health. We decided that in addition to the patient mobile app, we would develop a web app that would allow the doctor, lab technician, or nurse to import blood work data to the user’s account and also export heart health data from the Pulseband.

Through our research we also determined that people have a high level of compliance with simple daily tasks such as taking blood pressure medication every day. In addition, we discovered that more knowledge does not necessarily lead to behaviour change. With this in mind, we decided to pair health information with actionable simple steps to empower the user to improve their health.

Following our research synthesis we developed user personas, our problem statement, and design principles.

Problem Statement:
How might we develop a holistic heart health monitor that enables patients to understand their heart health in a way that motivates and empowers them to make healthy lifestyle choices.

Wireframes & Prototyping

The patient app included three tabs: Dashboard, My Health Data, and Challenges. I developed the wireframes and prototypes for the Challenge portion of the patient app, which I will focus on below. The challenges section of the app uses the user’s current health data to suggest simple healthy activities for the user to complete on a daily basis.

In addition to the Challenges portion of the patient app, I also developed the wireframes and prototypes for the medical professional web app. The web app includes a main dashboard screen allowing the medical professional to import/export data, view current data and trends, and examine abnormal EKG data.

Using mid-fidelity prototypes, I conducted user testing sessions to test our designs. The following iteration included more customization in the Challenges section, and the web app was refined to reduce the complexity of the feature set.
